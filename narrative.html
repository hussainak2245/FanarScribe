Narrative pitch

Clinical intelligence that listens, writes, and knows when to ask.

SAJIL is built for the moment after a busy outpatient consultation, when the physician still has to translate the conversation into a clean clinical record.

Listen Understand Ground Ask Finalize

01

The visit is short. The record is not.

Outpatient doctors work through high patient flow, multilingual conversations, and compressed consultation time. Documentation turns every visit into extra work.

02

Arabic clinical conversations are not clean transcripts.

Patients describe symptoms in dialect, switch into English medical terms, and use everyday phrases that can carry clinical meaning. Translation-first tools miss this texture.

03

The market is ready, but Qatar needs a local workflow.

DAX and Scribeberry show global demand. Avey, QCRI, and Fanar show Qatar and the region already have serious health-AI and Arabic-AI building blocks.

04

SAJIL connects the pieces.

It turns consultation input into structured notes, uses retrieval to ground output in trusted references, and flags unclear details before they become part of the record.

05

The doctor stays in the loop.

If a symptom, medication, diagnosis, or phrase is uncertain, SAJIL asks. The final note remains physician-reviewed, physician-corrected, and physician-approved.
